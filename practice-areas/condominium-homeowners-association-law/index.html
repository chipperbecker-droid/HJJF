Hudson, Jones, Jaywork & Fisher provides Delaware legal counsel to homeowners associations and condominium associations on community governance, financial stewardship, and legal compliance. The firm helps boards and property managers maintain operational stability, protect property values, and address disputes within common-interest communities.

The firm assists associations with delinquent dues and assessment collection, demand letters, pre-lien notices, liens, foreclosure actions where appropriate, personal judgment lawsuits, payment plans, and settlement agreements. These collection efforts are handled with attention to applicable debt collection and consumer protection requirements.

The firm also drafts, amends, interprets, and restates governing documents, including declarations, bylaws, deed restrictions, covenants, rules, and other association documents. The firm advises on Delaware Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act questions, board meetings, elections, developer transitions, reserve funding, insurance obligations, maintenance responsibilities, and owner disputes.

Delaware HOA and Condominium FAQ

What legal issues do Delaware associations face?

Common issues include assessment collection, covenant enforcement, governing document interpretation, board governance, meetings, elections, maintenance obligations, developer transitions, and owner disputes.

What is DUCIOA?

DUCIOA is the Delaware Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act. It can affect condominium and homeowners association governance, disclosures, assessments, and owner rights depending on the community and the issue.

Can an association collect delinquent assessments?

Associations may have options such as demand letters, liens, payment plans, litigation, or other enforcement measures, depending on the governing documents and applicable law.
