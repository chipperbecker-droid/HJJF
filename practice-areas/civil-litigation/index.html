Hudson, Jones, Jaywork & Fisher represents clients in Delaware civil disputes that may involve contractual breaches, property disputes, personal injuries, construction matters, business torts, and other contentious matters. When a disagreement escalates, skilled and assertive legal representation can be critical to protecting a client's rights and interests.

The firm's civil litigation attorneys handle breach of contract disputes, property disputes involving boundaries or easements, personal injury claims, business torts such as fraud or misrepresentation, unfair competition, defamation, tortious interference, construction disputes, and disputes between neighbors. In contract matters, the firm evaluates available remedies such as damages, specific performance, injunctive relief, or other equitable relief when appropriate.

Delaware Litigation FAQ

What kinds of civil cases does the firm handle in Delaware?

The firm handles contract disputes, property disputes, business torts, construction matters, personal injury claims, neighbor disputes, and other civil litigation in Delaware courts.

Can a civil dispute be resolved without trial?

Many disputes are resolved through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or motion practice. Trial may be necessary when the parties cannot reach a fair resolution.

What is a breach of contract claim in Delaware?

A breach of contract claim generally involves an agreement, an alleged failure to perform under that agreement, and claimed damages or other requested relief. The specific rights and remedies depend on the contract and the facts of the dispute.
