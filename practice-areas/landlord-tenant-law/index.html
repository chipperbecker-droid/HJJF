Hudson, Jones, Jaywork & Fisher represents landlords, tenants, property owners, and property managers in Delaware commercial and residential landlord-tenant matters. These matters are governed by leases, Delaware statutes, local requirements, and court procedures, and they often require prompt and practical legal guidance.

For landlords and property managers, the firm assists with summary possession actions, non-payment of rent disputes, holdover tenancies, lease drafting and review, security deposit issues, property damage claims, and compliance with housing regulations. The firm helps clients evaluate notice requirements, lease breaches, past-due rent, holdover damages, and lawful possession issues.

The firm also represents tenants in disputes involving eviction defense, security deposit recovery, habitability issues, lease interpretation, landlord harassment, illegal entry concerns, and negotiation of lease terms or renewals.

Delaware Landlord-Tenant FAQ

What is summary possession in Delaware?

Summary possession is the court process used in many Delaware eviction matters to resolve possession of a rental property. The facts, notice requirements, lease terms, and court deadlines matter.

Can a Delaware landlord recover unpaid rent?

A landlord may have options to pursue unpaid rent, possession, damages, or settlement depending on the lease, notices, property condition, and court procedure.

Can tenants dispute security deposit deductions?

Tenants may dispute improper deductions or return issues. Lease terms, move-in and move-out records, damage documentation, and Delaware law may all affect the analysis.
