Hudson, Jones, Jaywork & Fisher represents individuals who have suffered harm due to negligence or other wrongful conduct in Delaware. Serious accidents can create physical, emotional, and financial burdens, and the firm works to help clients pursue out-of-pocket costs, lost wages, medical expenses, and fair compensation for pain and suffering.

The firm handles motor vehicle accident claims involving cars, trucks, motorcycles, pedestrians, cyclists, distracted driving, impaired driving, reckless driving, and uninsured or underinsured motorists. The firm gathers evidence, reviews police reports and medical records, evaluates insurance coverage, and works to identify responsible parties.

The firm also assists with injury matters involving unsafe property conditions, medical negligence, workplace-related accidents that may involve third-party claims, animal attacks, defective products, wrongful death claims, and other negligence-related injuries.

Delaware Personal Injury FAQ

What should I do after an accident in Delaware?

Seek medical attention, report the accident when appropriate, preserve photos and records, notify insurance carriers as required, and speak with an attorney before making recorded statements or signing releases.

What damages may be involved in a Delaware injury claim?

Depending on the facts, a claim may involve medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, out-of-pocket costs, and other losses tied to the injury.

Does the firm handle uninsured motorist claims?

Yes. The firm assists with claims involving uninsured and underinsured motorists, including review of available insurance coverage and negotiation with insurers.
